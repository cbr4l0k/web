Okay, there is a substance in grass and some vegetables called beta-carotene; in short, it is what gives butter its yellow color. It is fat-soluble; therefore, when the fat separates from the butter-milk, the beta-carotene stays with the fat. When butter looks more yellow, it is because the cows were fed fresh grass instead of hay, silage, or grain.
