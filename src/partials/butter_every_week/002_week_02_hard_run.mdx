Today was weird. I entered the cafe, and I had way more energy than everyone around me. I was feeling good. I woke up late and arrived early anyway... I was so ready to run until the moment the run started hahahaha.

WTF happened?????

Everyone was going so fast, or was I slow? From an early moment, I was able to understand this was going to be a challenging run. So the mindset was to continue, slower if necessary, but <b>NO STOPPING</b> until the run was over.

At some point, I saw other great runners falling behind like me, and that helped me understand it was not just me... was it us? or was it them?

I decided to support the last one and assume that they were just going really fast. I pushed myself to go as fast as them for as long as possible. This might have happened after the second half of the run ~(no idea where, because I don't track my runs)~.

My legs were hurting the whole time, and I'm proud to say that at no point did I have thoughts of stopping. All the time, my mindset was clear: forward, until the end.

And the end arrived. With <code>13.58 km</code> and an average pace of <code>5'33"</code>, I'm able to say: butter is ready. After rinsing and storing it, I took some coffee for me and for Sveta in my thermos, packed a banana and some cookies for her, and left for her house to store the butter.

Butter was in my mind most of the day. So at 20:00, left the University, bought some bread, and went to her place to taste the butter.

Was amazing, I'll try to keep this habit for as long as possible, or at least until it stops being interesting.
